<p><p>Heat allocators are devices used to measure and distribute heating energy in multi-unit buildings. The evaporating style heat allocator uses a liquid-filled chamber that evaporates fluid based on heat exposure, allowing precise measurement of energy consumption. In contrast, electric heat allocators utilize electronic sensors to monitor temperature variations, providing real-time data on energy usage. Both market types play a crucial role in promoting energy efficiency, enabling accurate billing among tenants, and fostering a sustainable approach to heating management.</p></p>
