<p><ul><li>Ultrasound Method</li><li>Optical Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pachymeter device market is primarily segmented into two types: the ultrasound method and the optical method. The ultrasound method utilizes sound waves to measure corneal thickness, providing accurate and reliable results, particularly in clinical settings. In contrast, the optical method employs light to assess corneal thickness, offering a non-invasive approach that is often faster and easier for users. Both methods cater to the growing demand for precise ophthalmic diagnostics, driven by increasing cases of eye disorders and advancements in technology.</p></p>
