<p><p>The mesophilic dairy starter market is categorized into single strain and compound strains. Single strain starters consist of one specific bacterial species, providing consistent fermentation characteristics and flavors. In contrast, compound strains blend multiple bacterial cultures, enhancing complexity, flavor profiles, and fermentation efficiencies. Single strains are often preferred for traditional cheese and yogurt production, while compound strains are favored for their ability to produce diverse flavors and textures, catering to a wider range of dairy products and consumer preferences.</p></p>
