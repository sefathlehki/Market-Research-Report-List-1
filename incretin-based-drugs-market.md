<p><ul><li>Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor (GLP-1) agonists</li><li>Dipeptidyl Peptidase-4 (DPP-4) inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Incretin-based drugs are used to improve glycemic control in diabetes management, primarily through two main types: GLP-1 receptor agonists and DPP-4 inhibitors. GLP-1 receptor agonists mimic the incretin hormone, enhancing insulin secretion, reducing glucagon release, and promoting satiety, leading to weight loss. Conversely, DPP-4 inhibitors block the enzyme that breaks down incretins, increasing their levels, which also supports insulin release and lowers blood sugar levels. Both classes contribute significantly to the diabetes treatment landscape, each with unique benefits and mechanisms.</p></p>
