<p><p>The Atrial Appendage Occluder market is primarily divided into two types based on the type of atrial fibrillation: valvular and non-valvular. Valvular atrial fibrillation occurs in patients with heart valve disease, necessitating occluders to prevent thrombus formation. In contrast, non-valvular atrial fibrillation arises in the absence of significant valvular heart disease, primarily in patients with other risk factors. These classifications guide treatment options, market strategies, and product development in the growing atrial fibrillation management sector.</p></p>
